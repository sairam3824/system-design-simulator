# Resume Features

## Overview

The resume feature allows you to upload your resume for analysis, get ATS (Applicant Tracking System) scoring, and receive personalized interview recommendations.

## Uploading Your Resume

1. Navigate to "Resume" in the sidebar
2. Click "Upload Resume" or drag and drop your file
3. Supported formats: PDF, DOC, DOCX
4. Wait for the analysis to complete

## ATS Score

Your resume receives an ATS score from 0-100 based on:

### Scoring Breakdown

1. **Contact Information (10%)**
   - Name clearly visible
   - Email address
   - Phone number
   - LinkedIn profile
   - Location (city/state)

2. **Structure & Formatting (20%)**
   - Clear section headings
   - Consistent formatting
   - Appropriate length (1-2 pages)
   - Professional layout
   - ATS-friendly format

3. **Experience (30%)**
   - Relevant work history
   - Clear job titles
   - Company names
   - Employment dates
   - Progression shown

4. **Keywords (25%)**
   - Technical skills mentioned
   - Industry-relevant terms
   - Job-specific keywords
   - Tools and technologies

5. **Impact (15%)**
   - Quantified achievements
   - Action verbs used
   - Results demonstrated
   - Metrics included

## Personalized Interviews

Your resume data enhances your interview experience:

### Smart Difficulty Adjustment
- Experience level affects difficulty
- Skills influence topic complexity
- Past performance considered

### Tailored Questions
- Questions relate to your background
- Focus on relevant technologies
- Consider target companies

### Topic Recommendations
- Topics matching your experience
- Areas to strengthen
- Company-specific suggestions

## Best Practices

### For Better ATS Scores
1. Use standard section headings
2. Include relevant keywords naturally
3. Quantify achievements
4. Use a clean, simple format
5. Avoid tables and graphics
6. Save as PDF when possible

### For Better Interviews
1. Keep resume current
2. Highlight system design experience
3. List relevant technologies
4. Include scale metrics (users, requests)
5. Show leadership experience

## Re-analyzing Your Resume

After updating your resume:
1. Upload the new version
2. Previous versions are kept for reference
3. Compare scores over time
4. See improvement suggestions

## Privacy

- Resumes are stored securely
- Only you can access your resume
- Data used only for personalization
- You can delete anytime
